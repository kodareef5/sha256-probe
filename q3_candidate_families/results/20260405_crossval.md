# Cross-Validation: dW[61] Constant vs SAT Solve Time

## Experiment

Test 8 candidates at reduced word widths N=10, 12, 14.
Each gets a 300s Kissat timeout per width.

## Key Finding

**dW[61] constant HW does NOT predict reduced-width SAT solve time.**

- The dW61=12 candidate (best by this metric) is the SLOWEST at N=10 (123s)
  and times out at N=12.
- The dW61=18 candidate (worst by this metric) is among the FASTEST at both
  N=10 (24.8s) and N=12 (66.0s).
- Rank correlation between dW61_C and N=10 solve time: **negative** (higher
  dW61_C correlates with FASTER solving, opposite of hypothesis).

## Interpretation

The dW[61] constant is a 32-bit metric computed from the full-width schedule.
At reduced widths (N=10-14), rotation amounts are scaled, constants are
truncated, and carry chains are shorter. The mini-SHA at N=10 is a
qualitatively different hash function, and 32-bit metrics don't transfer.

The candidates that solve fastest at reduced widths (0x3f239926, 0x44b49bc3)
may have favorable properties that are NOT captured by any of our current
32-bit metrics. Understanding WHY they're fast could lead to better
candidate selection strategies.

## Evidence Level

**EVIDENCE**: dW[61] constant HW is NOT a reliable predictor of sr=60
difficulty across word widths. Tested on 8 candidates × 3 widths.

**HYPOTHESIS**: The fastest candidates at reduced widths (0x44b49bc3,
0x3f239926) have some structural property that makes them favorable.
Identifying this property is the key open question.
