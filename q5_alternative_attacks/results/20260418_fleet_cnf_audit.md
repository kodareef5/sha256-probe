# Fleet CNF Audit: ALL 38 "sr=61" CNFs are actually sr=60

**Date**: 2026-04-18
**Evidence level**: VERIFIED (exhaustive check)

## Finding

ALL 38 CNFs in cnfs_n32/ with the "sr61_" filename prefix are actually
sr=60 encodings (4 free words including W[60]). Zero are true sr=61
(3 free words with W[60] schedule-determined).

## How We Verified

Checked whether SAT variables 194-257 (which would be W1[60] and W2[60]
free variables) appear in any clauses:
- If vars 194-257 appear: sr=60 (W[60] is free)
- If vars 194-257 don't appear: sr=61 (W[60] schedule-determined)

Result: all 38 CNFs have vars 194-257 → all are sr=60.

## Impact

- The ~2000+ CPU-hours of "sr=61 racing" across the fleet were spent
  on sr=60 instances
- The bit19 SAT result is a valid sr=60 collision, not sr=61
- True sr=61 at N=32 has NEVER been attempted with these candidates
- The 8 cascade-augmented CNFs (sr61_cascade_*) are ALSO sr=60 misnamed
  (they add cascade constraints but still have 4 free words)

## Root Cause

The CNFs were generated by encode_sr60_kernel.py (4 free words) but
saved with filenames containing "sr61_". The naming convention was
inconsistent with the encoding.

2. Launch actual sr=61 seeds on freed cores
3. Notify fleet about the naming error
