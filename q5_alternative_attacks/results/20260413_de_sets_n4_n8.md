## Key Finding: de57, de59, de60 are CONSTANT

For a given candidate, de57 is the SAME for all w57 values:
```
de57 = (d1-d2) + (h1-h2) + (Sig1(e1)-Sig1(e2)) + (Ch1-Ch2) + (K-K) + (w1-w2)
     = (state56 diffs) + (-cascade_offset)
     = CONSTANT (depends only on state56)
```

This means de57 cannot be used for FORWARD pruning — every w57 passes the de57 check because de57 is the same for all w57.

However, de58 varies because state57 (input to round 58) depends on w57.
Of 256 possible de58 values, only 8 appear across all 260 collisions.

## Scaling extrapolation

| N | |de57| | |de58| | |de59| | |de60| | Product | log2 |
|---|--------|--------|--------|--------|---------|------|
| 4 | 1 | 2 | 1 | 1 | 2 | 1.0 |

de58 scales as roughly 2^(N/2.7). If this holds:
- N=10: |de58| ≈ 14 → product ≈ 14 → 2^3.8 effective
- N=32: |de58| ≈ 2^12 → product ≈ 4096 → 2^12 effective vs 2^128

This suggests the de-pruning gives ~2^(N/2.7) effective search,
which is exponentially better than 2^(4N) brute force.
