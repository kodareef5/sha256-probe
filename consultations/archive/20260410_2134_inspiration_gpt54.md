# 3. Variants of your current approaches you should try differently

## 3.1 SAT encodings

### A. Stop using pure dual-copy bit-blast as the main representation
Use:
- one-copy state,
- differential vars,
- explicit carries,
- native XOR clauses where possible.

If you keep linear layers as XORs, CryptoMiniSat becomes relevant again. Right now you’re probably destroying too much linear structure in CNF.

---

### B. Expose carries explicitly and branch on them
If carries are implicit inside full-adder Tseitin blobs, the solver has to rediscover the arithmetic structure. Don’t make it.

Try:
- one variable per carry bit,
- carry-save adder decomposition for T1/T2,
- and branching priority on high-impact carries.

Your own custom CSA-tree result at sr=59 already points this way.

---

### C. Use cube-and-conquer, not seed superstition
A 12-hour SAT with seed dependence means you have a branching problem, not a mystical hard instance.

Try:
- cubes on carry bits,
- cubes on schedule-critical bits extracted from UNSAT cores,
- parallel portfolio on cubes, not raw seeds.

---

### D. Add symmetry breaking
At minimum:
- order the two messages lexicographically on the first differing word/bit,
- or fix one differential bit orientation.

You’re otherwise paying a factor ~2 for swap symmetry and maybe more for trivial equivalents.

---

### E. Incremental SAT with activation literals
Use one master formula and sweep:
- candidate assumptions,
- kernel positions,
- schedule-bit enforcement subsets,
- near-collision objectives.

Learned clauses can transfer. Rebuilding per candidate is waste.

---

### F. Try AIG/ABC preprocessing before CNF
Structural hashing and logic rewriting often shrink ARX circuits more than hand intuition expects.

Pipeline:
- build AIG,
- `fraig`, `rewrite`, `dc2`,
- then CNF.

It can expose equivalences and reduce adder clutter.

---

## 3.2 Algebraic representations

### A. Stop fetishizing component ANFs
Component ANFs are descriptive, not automatically useful.

Instead try:
- vectorial combinations,
- common annihilators of multiple equations,
- elimination ideals after fixing a carry skeleton,
- and degree after coordinate change.

---

### B. Use carry-conditioned linear algebra
Condition on a subset of carries, then solve the remaining XOR system with Gaussian elimination.

That’s the algebraic representation that actually matches ARX structure.

---

### C. If you do Gröbner, do hybrid Gröbner
Naive F4/F5 on raw SHA-256 equations is almost certainly a dead end.

Only try Gröbner after:
- restricting to exact-cone subproblems,
- fixing a chunk of carries,
- and maybe restricting to 8–12 key variables.

Otherwise you will just burn time.

Tools worth installing:
- Sage + PolyBoRi,
- Singular,
- maybe Bosphorus for ANF/SAT interplay.

---

### D. Use projected model counting / #SAT
For N=8/10/12 and restricted exact-cone models:
- count projected solutions over message vars,
- count by carry signature,
- estimate entropy loss per added schedule bit.

This is much more meaningful than affine span or random correlation.

## 3.3 Search strategies

### A. Replace output-HW search with structural deficit search
Score a sample by:
- number of satisfied zeroing conditions,

Not by final HW.

### B. Do guided exhaustive search where dimensionality collapses

This is standard collision-path engineering. You should do it.

# 4. Mathematical frameworks you’re missing

## 4.1 Carry automata / finite-state transducer viewpoint
This is the cleanest missing framework.

Model the tail as:
- permutations/linear maps,
- plus a small set of additions,
- whose nonlinearity is entirely in carry transitions.

Then the hard part is a finite-state consistency problem over carry interfaces.

What this predicts:
- the sr=60/61 boundary may correspond to a **branching factor crossing 1** in the carry automaton after one more schedule coupling is imposed;
- “phase transition” should be measured by surviving carry-state count, not SAT time.

This is probably your best route to an actual explanation.

---

## 4.2 Division property / monomial propagation
Use it to answer:
- why h and d are weak,
- why some variables are absent,
- what degree upper bounds are forced by topology,
- and whether the observed sparsity is surprising.

It gives a principled replacement for the current ANF storytelling.

---

## 4.3 Elimination ideals / hybrid algebraic cryptanalysis
Not full raw Gröbner.  
Hybrid elimination:
- fix some carries,
- project to the message variables or differential variables,
- eliminate aggressively,
- look for low-degree consequences.

What it predicts:
- most of your “weak” output bits probably vanish as useful objects after elimination;
- the real algebraic simplification lives in conditioned subproblems.

---

## 4.4 Vectorial Boolean function analysis
You’re treating the tail as 32 separate bits too often.  
It’s a vectorial map. Study:
- degree of linear combinations,
- Walsh spectrum on masks,
- autocorrelation of derivatives,
- algebraic immunity of combinations,
- normality on restricted subspaces.

That is the correct language if you want “exploitability,” not raw component sparsity.

---

## 4.5 Knowledge compilation / exact counting
This is the right way to replace vague “landscape” metaphors.

Compute:
- exact/projection counts of surviving assignments after each added constraint,
- by carry signature, round, or enforced schedule bits.

Then you can say:
- sr=60 has X surviving structured branches,
- sr=61 collapses at constraint Y,
- and the collapse is due to Z.

That’s an explanation. SAT runtime is not.

---

## 4.6 What theory predicts about your observed structure

### Why h is weak
Register age + fewer fresh T1/T2 injections + limited carry cone.

### Why upper bits of W60 don’t affect h[0]
No carry path into bit 0 in the available structure.

### Why degree grows fast elsewhere
ARX addition and Ch/Maj composition saturate degree quickly once a register re-enters nonlinear additions multiple times.

### Why mini-SHA may show a sharp boundary
Because your rounded rotation family may accidentally preserve or kill a local zeroing mechanism uniformly across widths. That says little about actual 32-bit SHA-256.

---

## 4.7 Frameworks that are mostly not useful here

### Lattice theory / LLL / BKZ
No. This is not a near-linear integer relation problem.

### Number theory
Outside of 2-adic language for carries, basically no.

### Boomerang/rectangle
Mostly wrong problem class. Good for block ciphers/permutations with split trails; not a natural fit for exact one-block SHA-256 collision constraints.

### Generic ML on solutions
With 49 tiny toy solutions and strong symbolic structure, ML is cargo cult unless used for:
- branching heuristics,
- candidate ranking from engineered features,
- or backdoor prediction.

They are cheap but low-value.

Randomly firing 10B samples at a 2^128 parametrization is mostly self-soothing.

Those are distractions.

---

## Stop 7: Presenting timeout artifacts as candidate uniqueness
“Only 0x17149975 cracked” is not science until you have:

It isn’t.
