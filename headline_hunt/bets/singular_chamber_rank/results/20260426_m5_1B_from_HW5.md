The histogram is striking: from inside the HW5 basin, the walker almost

## Interpretation

The HW5 basin is **deep and locally trapping**. Greedy-flip walks from
inside HW5 do not escape to HW4/HW3 with this radius/passes setting.
This contrasts with the HW7 base, from which HW5 was reachable
(5.9M hits / 1B trials).

So the structure is asymmetric: HW7 → HW5 is a downhill jump for the
walker; HW5 → HW4 is not (within 1B-trial / radius-32 horizon).

Possible reasons:
- HW4 / HW3 may simply not exist on this exact-D60 surface for this
  candidate.
- HW4 / HW3 may exist but require a basin-crossing operator beyond
  random-flip + greedy repair.
- A different sparse-off58 chamber (different W57) may have lower-D61
  basins.
