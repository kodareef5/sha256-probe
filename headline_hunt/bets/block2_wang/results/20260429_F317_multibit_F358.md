seed init) but mutator extended to include 2-bit and 3-bit combined flips
on (active_word, bit) pairs:

- 4/20 prob: 1-bit single-word flip (was the only mode in F315)

## Findings

### Finding 1 — Multibit moves do NOT break the a57=4 floor

F315 (1-bit moves, F358 seed) reached a57=4. F317 (1+2+3-bit moves, F358
seed) reached only a57=6. **The multibit mutator's average move is
"larger" in atlas-loss space**, often jumping over good 1-bit basins
without converging back. The increased move size hurts fine-grained
descent at the boundary.

### Finding 2 — Multibit moves DO improve chart coherence

8/8 restarts in (dh, dCh)/(dCh, dh) chart at best (vs F315's 5/8). Average
chart matches per restart up 73% (220 vs 127). The multibit moves let
the search escape non-chamber chart pockets that 1-bit moves can get
trapped in.

### Finding 3 — Multibit + 1-bit at right ratio is the natural extension

Pure 1-bit (F315): converges fine, gets stuck in chart pockets.
Mostly multibit (F317): escapes chart pockets, can't converge fine.

Better: ANNEAL the multibit ratio. Early in search, use multibit (escape
chart pockets); late in search, use 1-bit (fine convergence). Worth ~5
minutes implementation.

### Finding 4 — Chamber attractor still unreached

0/8 chamber_hits. Confirmed: neither 1-bit (F315/F316) nor multibit (F317)
moves on dM reach the chamber attractor from chamber-adjacent init at
8×20k. The basin is genuinely small and isolated.

## What this means

Three structural mechanisms tested in sequence (F312 random init, F315
chamber seed, F317 multibit) and none reach the chamber attractor. The
remaining options are:

(a) **Annealed mutator**: multibit early, 1-bit late. Best of both worlds.
(b) **Larger active mask** (all 16 words): more freedom for the search.
(c) **Direct (W57, W58, W59) moves on M1**: search modifies M1 such that
    schedule produces target W's. Avoids the dM-mutator entirely.
(d) **Yale longer free-var run** (1M steps with atlas-aware obj): drive
    true_mismatch_hw < 20 BEFORE handing to atlas-loss search. Yale's
    side of the cross-machine flywheel.

Most concrete + cheap: (a) annealed mutator. Most likely to actually break:
(d), waiting on yale.
