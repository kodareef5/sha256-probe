## Findings

### Finding 1 — Longer iters give broader chart coverage but not deeper basin dives

F319's 50k iters hit ALL 8 restarts in chamber chart with average chart_matches
of 539 (+130% vs F318's 234). But the unusual F318 r2 result (D61=7 in chamber
chart) is NOT reproduced in F319's 8 restarts at 50k each. The deep-basin event
that produced D61=7 appears to be relatively rare under this mutator.

### Finding 2 — F318 r2's D61=7 stands as the best chamber-chart point

Across F312 + F315-F319 (40 total restarts of various mechanisms, ~1.4M
iterations from chamber-seeded init), F318 r2's `(a57=8, D61=7, chart=(dh,dCh))`
remains the closest to the chamber attractor in the chart-coherent slice.

### Finding 3 — Rare-basin landscape suggests broader restart fan, not longer iters

Implication: invest compute in MORE RESTARTS at 20k each rather than fewer at
50k. F319's 50k×8 = 400k iters didn't find anything F318's 20k×8 = 160k
didn't find. Better budget allocation: 32 restarts at 20k each = 640k iters
with much broader basin sampling.

### Finding 4 — Chamber attractor still unreached

40 restarts × ~25k average iters ≈ 1M total iters across all seeded mechanisms.
Zero chamber_hits. Confirms: the chamber attractor is structurally unreachable
from chamber-adjacent dM single+multi-bit mutation, regardless of compute scaling.

The remaining bottleneck is NOT compute. It is mechanism: we need either
(a) yale to deliver a TRUE chamber-on M1 (true_mismatch_hw=0), or
(b) a structural change to the search like chamber-pinning the chart explicitly.
