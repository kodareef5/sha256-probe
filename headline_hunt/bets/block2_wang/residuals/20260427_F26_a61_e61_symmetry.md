# F26: a_61 ⊕ e_61 symmetry pattern across 5 distinguished cands
**2026-04-27 04:25 EDT**

Investigates whether F24's "bit13_m4e560940 has a_61 = e_61 exactly"
finding is bit13-specific or a broader pattern across distinguished
cands.

## Result

Per F25's per-cand min-HW residual vectors, extract positions 2 (a_61)
and 6 (e_61) and compute XOR overlap:

| cand | a_61 | e_61 | a_61 ⊕ e_61 | HW |
|---|---|---|---|---:|
| **bit13_m4e560940** | 0x00820042 | 0x00820042 | **0x00000000** | **0** |
| idx8_m33ec77ca | 0x18288001 | 0x08288001 | 0x10000000 | 1 |
| msb_m189b13c7 | 0x10643c00 | 0x30243c00 | 0x20400000 | 2 |
| msb_m17149975 | 0x00080016 | 0x0038001a | 0x0030000c | 4 |
| bit18_m99bf552b | 0x00004700 | 0x0001c500 | 0x00018200 | 4 |

**bit13 is the ONLY cand with EXACT a_61 = e_61 symmetry.** All others
have 1-4 bits of asymmetry.

## Interpretation

In SHA-256's state vector at slot 64 = `[a_63, a_62, a_61, a_60, e_63,
e_62, e_61, e_60]`:
- Position 2 (a_61) and position 6 (e_61) are register positions THREE
  rounds apart in the shift register.
- a_61 results from round-61 operations on the original a-register.
- e_61 results from round-61 operations on the e-register, which
  receives e_new = d + T1 = (state[3] + T1).

For their XOR difference to be ZERO means the round-61 a-register and
e-register diffs are IDENTICAL bit patterns despite differing source
operations.

Algebraically: a_61_diff = b_60_diff + (T1+T2)_61 differential, while
e_61_diff = a_60_diff + T1_61 differential. Under cascade-1, b_60 =
a_59 (shift) so b_60_diff = a_59_diff and a_60_diff = 0 (cascade-1
holds at slot 60).

For bit13, the specific algebra produces a_61_diff = e_61_diff. For
other cands, the algebra produces 1-4 bit asymmetry.

## Implication for block2_wang

The a_61 = e_61 symmetry is a **structural feature** that may admit
**simultaneous cancellation** via a single bit-pattern absorption
operator on slot-61 components. For Wang-style absorption:
- bit13: 1 absorption pattern handles BOTH a_61 and e_61 (8-bit
  pattern × 2 components = 16 bits absorbed by 8-bit operator).
- Other cands: 2 absorption patterns needed (one for a_61, one for
  e_61), with bit-asymmetry being the cost.

This is structurally a SECOND distinguishing feature of bit13_m4e560940,
on top of:
- F12 cascade-1 chamber image min HW=3 (rank 2 of registry)
- F17/F18/F25 round-63 residual min HW=47 (rank 2 of distinguished set)
- F26 (this) a_61 = e_61 symmetry (UNIQUE in distinguished set)

For block2_wang's Wang-style attack, bit13 may admit shorter absorption
sequences than other cands.

## What this rules in / out

- **Rules in**: bit13_m4e560940 has structurally distinct a_61=e_61
  symmetry not found in 4 other distinguished cands.
- **Doesn't rule out**: similar symmetry exists in some cand outside
  the 5-cand distinguished set. Full registry F25-style scan would
  test this.
