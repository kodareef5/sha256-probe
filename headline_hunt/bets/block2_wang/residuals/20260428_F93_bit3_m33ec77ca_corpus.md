## Structural finding: kernel + fill density predicts corpus yield

| Kernel type | Fill density | Records ≤ HW=80 |
|---|---|---:|
| bit-N (N=2, 3, 13) | dense (0xff... or 0xaa...) | 18k |
| MSB (bit-31) | sparse (0x80... or 0x00...) | 3-4k |

The bit-N kernels with dense fills produce ~5× more low-HW residuals
than MSB kernels with sparse fills. This is consistent with F26's
finding that bit13 has structurally rigid residuals, and extends it:
**dense-fill bit-N kernels in general show this property**. The MSB
kernel with sparse fill has fewer low-HW paths because the cascade
trigger is more localized.
