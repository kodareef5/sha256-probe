# de58 + de59 stacked hints — 1.87× median, 0% regressions (n=18)
**2026-04-26 19:30 EDT**

Extends de58-at-w57 (commit d0d35e2) with a free de59 hint stack.
Substantially better on every dimension.

## Two structural facts

1. **Per-chamber de58_size = 1** (commit 3bc3da9): at fixed W57,
   de58 is one specific 32-bit value. Chamber-specific.

2. **de59 is cand-level invariant** (verified: 64 random W57s →
   exactly 1 distinct de59 per cand for 5 tested cands):

   | cand | de59 (chamber-invariant) |
   |---|---|
   | bit19 | 0xf9f7f920 |
   | idx0  | 0x754fbd5d |
   | idx8  | 0x0156ad16 |
   | idx17 | 0x94f1f396 |
   | idx3  | 0x99c3f2f4 |

   So de59 hints can be injected with NO W57 cost — they're free
   per-cand information. This works because under cascade-1, the
   slot-58 inputs to round-59 satisfy da[58]=0, which makes
   dT2_59 = 0 and dT1_59 = 0, so de59 = de57 (cand-determined).

   **Mechanism**: cascade-1 propagates da-zero across slots, which
   makes Sigma0 and Maj differences vanish at slot k+1. The "de"
   chain then becomes a delayed copy of earlier de values — purely
   cand-level after the first cascade-1 step.

3. **de60, de61, de62, de63 are structurally 0** under cascade-1.
   These slots don't add new info per chamber.

## Why de59 stacks cleanly with de58

de58 chamber-fix prunes W57 to ~2^24 / 2^32 valid choices. de59
cand-level fix doesn't restrict W57 at all (it's a constant on the
cand). They constrain different solver paths:
- de58: "given which W57 chamber are you in, use this chamber's de58."
- de59: "regardless of W57, de59 is always THIS value."

The combination tells the solver de58 is X (chamber-dependent) AND
de59 is Y (cand-fixed) — two compatible constraints on adjacent
state-vector slots. Solver propagates more aggressively from the
chamber-W57 search to the de58/de59 outputs.

## What's still open

- **Mode B + de58 + de59 stack**: would the strongest expose-side
  hints stack with Mode B's force-side semantics? Risk: collide.
  Expected: Mode B is solution-set restriction, de58/de59 are
  unit-propagation hints — orthogonal mechanisms, should compose.
  Worth testing.

- **de60..de63 redundant zeros**: structurally 0 under cascade-1,
  but the encoder's Mode A may not propagate this efficiently from
  the cascade clauses alone. Adding 4×32=128 unit clauses fixing
  de60..de63 to 0 might give marginal extra speedup with zero
  W57 commitment cost.

- **Higher conflict budgets**: the 13-bit hint's speedup decayed
  by ~500k. Stacked hints encode more information; speedup may
  decay slower or hold longer.

- **F3 follow-up: Mode A wall predictor on stacked**. Likely
  weaker than de58-only (predictor weakens as hint strengthens).

EVIDENCE-level: VERIFIED at n=18 × 3 seeds × 50k. The mechanism for
both de58 chamber-dependence and de59 cand-invariance is structurally
