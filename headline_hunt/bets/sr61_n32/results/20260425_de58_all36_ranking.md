`headline_hunt/bets/sr61_n32/de58_all_candidates.py` — single-file Python, runs in ~80s for all 36 candidates.

## Three notable findings

### 1. bit-19_m51ca0b34 is a singular structural extreme

24 bits of de58 compression vs the next-best 20. **4 bits gap** to the second tier. This is not just rare — it's structurally distinctive. The Sigma1-aligned bit position (bit 19 in (i,j)=(0,9) word pair) plus fill=0x55555555 produces a cascade with extraordinary a-path concentration.

### 2. msb_m9cfea9ce_fill0x00000000 is a SURPRISE MSB candidate

This is an MSB-kernel candidate (bit-31, the same as the cert) but with fill=0x00000000 (vs the cert's 0xffffffff). The compression jumps from the cert's 15.2 bits to **20 bits** — 5 bit improvement just from changing fill.

**Implication**: choosing fill=0x00000000 may unlock structural concentration the cert candidate doesn't have. Worth multi-hour validation.

### 3. The MSB cert (m17149975, fill=0xff) is MEDIOCRE

15.2 bits compression — fits with the bottom-third of candidates. **The cert exists because it was found by ~12h SAT search at sr=60, NOT because the candidate is structurally promising for sr=61.**

This recasts the priority candidate's role: it's a known sr=60 SAT instance, useful for cross-validation, but **NOT the right candidate to seed an sr=61 search.**

## Recommended sr=61 SAT search ranking

If/when a multi-hour user-authorized search runs, prioritize in order:

1. **bit-19_m51ca0b34_fill55555555** (24 bits compression)
2. **msb_m9cfea9ce_fill00000000** (20 bits, MSB family) — SURPRISE candidate
3. **bit-25_m09990bd2_fill80000000** (20 bits)
4. **bit-11_m56076c68_fill55555555** (19 bits)
5. **bit-13_mbee3704b_fill00000000** (19 bits)

Skip the bottom 21 candidates (≤16 bits compression) until top-6 results are in.

## What this CHANGES for the bet portfolio

- BET.yaml#true_sr61_n32 now has a candidate-promise predictor.
- The "priority MSB cert" framing in old writeups is empirically obsolete for sr=61 — the cert is good for sr=60 cross-validation but mediocre for sr=61 promise.
- bit-19 emerges as the structural outlier deserving dedicated multi-hour attention.
- The msb_m9cfea9ce_fill0 surprise suggests fill choice matters as much as bit position — future kernel sweeps should explore (kernel-bit, fill) pairs more broadly.

## Caveats

- All measurements are cascade-DP-specific. Non-cascade approaches (Wang trail, kc_xor_d4) operate in different domains.
- Compression ≠ guaranteed SAT. It means SMALLER haystack, not GUARANTEED needle.
- 262k samples per candidate is enough for stable counts (verified via 16k vs 1M comparison earlier this session).
