## Notable: MSB cert candidate is one of the WORST

`cand_n32_msb_m17149975_fillffffffff` (the verified sr=60 cert) has 5 predicted dh60 hard bits — top of the high end. Yet kissat found this collision in 12h. This implies:
- Either kissat's structural reasoning is candidate-blind to dh60 hard-bit count, OR
- Other factors (f60, g60 hard bits; total joint hard-residue size) dominate solve difficulty.

The 35-candidate pre-screen uses ONLY dh60 (≤5 bits per candidate). The full hard-residue at round 60 includes f60 and g60 too (4 bits and ~18 bits respectively per `20260424_hard_residue_findings.md`). dh60 is the smallest contributor; f60 and g60 dominate the 28-bit total. So this dh60-only ranking is suggestive but not yet conclusive.

## What this enables

For any new TRUE sr=61 candidate (or fresh candidate generation): pre-screen by Δe57 in O(1) before committing compute. Smaller Δe57 hard-bit count → smaller MITM table key. Among the 35 we have, three candidates tie for minimum (2 bits) — these are the natural priority MITM targets if the bet pursues that route.

## Open

Extending to f60 (Δe59) and g60 (Δe58) requires deriving how the modular differences propagate through cw58 and cw59, which depend on (W[57]) and (W[57], W[58]) respectively. The Δs vary per (W[57], W[58]) realization, but the empirical hard-bit positions are stable — suggesting some marginalization gives a fixed prediction. Open research direction; ~1 day to derive.

## Reproduce

```python
import yaml, sys
sys.path.insert(0, '<REPO>')
from lib.sha256 import precompute_state, K, MASK, Sigma0, Sigma1, Ch, Maj

candidates = yaml.safe_load(open('<REPO>/headline_hunt/registry/candidates.yaml'))
for c in candidates:
    m0 = int(c['m0'], 16); fill = int(c['fill'], 16); kbit = c['kernel']['bit']
    M1=[m0]+[fill]*15; M2=list(M1); M2[0]^=1<<kbit; M2[9]^=1<<kbit
    s1,_=precompute_state(M1); s2,_=precompute_state(M2)
    if s1[0] != s2[0]: continue
    dh = (s1[7]-s2[7]) & MASK
    dSig1 = (Sigma1(s1[4])-Sigma1(s2[4])) & MASK
    dCh = (Ch(s1[4],s1[5],s1[6])-Ch(s2[4],s2[5],s2[6])) & MASK
    T2_1 = (Sigma0(s1[0])+Maj(s1[0],s1[1],s1[2])) & MASK
    T2_2 = (Sigma0(s2[0])+Maj(s2[0],s2[1],s2[2])) & MASK
    cw57 = (dh + dSig1 + dCh + (T2_1-T2_2)) & MASK
    dd56 = (s1[3]-s2[3]) & MASK
    de57_mod = (dd56 + dh + dSig1 + dCh - cw57) & MASK
    bits = [i for i in range(1,32) if 0.45 <= ((de57_mod & ((1<<i)-1)) / (1<<i)) <= 0.55]
    print(f"{c['id']:50s} h_count={len(bits)} bits={bits}")
```
